Every AI vendor wants you to believe their product is the answer. As a vendor-neutral partner, we don't — because the right answer is usually a mix, and it depends on your problem, not our catalogue.

Here's how we think about it.

## Buy when the problem is common

If your need is well-served by a mature tool — transcription, general chat assistants, standard document extraction — buy it. Building a worse version of a commodity is a waste of budget. Just check the deployment and data terms before you sign.

## Build when the edge is yours

The parts of your business that make you different are rarely on anyone's roadmap. That's where custom pays off: an agent that understands your operations, a workflow wired into systems only you run. You own it, and it compounds.

## Watch the lock-in

The expensive mistake isn't buy-vs-build — it's getting trapped. Prefer open formats, portable data, and architectures you could migrate off. A stack you can leave is a stack you can negotiate with.

## Usually: both

Most real deployments buy the commodity layer and build the differentiator on top. The skill is knowing which is which — and being honest when the honest answer costs the consultant a build.
